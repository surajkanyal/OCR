Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is a technology that enables the conversion of different types of documents, such as scanned paper documents, PDF files, or images captured by a digital camera, into editable and searchable data. It works by analyzing the patterns of light and dark areas in an image and converting them into text. OCR systems utilize advanced algorithms, including machine learning and computer vision techniques, to accurately recognize characters and words from images. This technology finds widespread applications in various industries, including document digitization, automated data entry, text extraction from images, and more. OCR plays a crucial role in streamlining document processing workflows, improving efficiency, and enabling businesses to extract valuable insights from large volumes of textual data.
